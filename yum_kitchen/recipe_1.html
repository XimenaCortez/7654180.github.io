Yum Yum Kitchen Fresh food done right!

Ingredients:

Serves: 4

  • 8.8/11.6 oz (250/330g) Pizza dough
  • 400g can of San Marzano plum tomatoes
  • Clove of garlic
  • Fresh basil
  • 1/2 tbsp dried oregano
  • Fine sea salt
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Cooking Intructions

  1. Prepare your pizza dought prior of time.
  2. Finely slice the garlic with a small serrated knife. Then, transfer it to a small bowl and cover it with a tbsp of olive oil.
  3. In a small bowl, dissolve 1/2 tsp salt in cold water. Consequently, pinch about 8 basil leaves and submerge them in the water(To prevent them from burning).
  4. Place the 400g can of San Marzano plum tomatoes in a bowl. And, add a generous pinch of sea salt. Next, use your hands to break the tomatoes down until you create a chunky sauce.
  5. Heat up your Ooni pizza oven. Traditional pizza needs a pizza stone to reacg high temperatures. Aim for 842°F(450°C).
  6. Using a small amount of flour, dust your pizza peel. Stretch the pizza dough ball out to 12"/16" and lay it out on your peel. Next, with a large spoon spread 5-6 tbsp of your pizza sauce evenly over the base(Be generous with the sauce).
  7. Add the slices of garlic, followed by the basil, (shake basil leaves to remove excess of water). Crush the oregano in the palms of your hand and sprinkle it across the centre of the pizza. Finally top with a swirl of olive oil and a pinch of fresh ground pepper.
  8. Slice the pizza off the peel and into your Ooni pizza oven. Let it cook for 1-2 minutes. Once cooked, remove it from the oven.
  9. Lastly, serve right away and enjoy this simple Marinara pizza.
